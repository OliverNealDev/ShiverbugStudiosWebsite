How it works

Slotting In, Without the Faff

No account managers, no six-week onboarding. You talk to the people doing the work, from the first email onwards.

STEP 01 Say hello

Email us a rough idea of what you need. A paragraph is plenty, and we're happy to sign an NDA before you share anything sensitive.

STEP 02 Scope it together

A call to pin down deliverables, timeline and budget. You'll get a clear written quote: fixed-price milestones or a day rate, whichever fits.

STEP 03 Start small

We recommend starting with a paid trial task, like one prop, one concept or one level blockout, so you can judge the fit before committing to more.

STEP 04 Slot in & ship

We work in your tools and your pipeline, with regular check-ins and one point of contact. You see progress every week, not just at milestones.

Questions, answered

The Stuff You'd Ask Anyway

What kinds of engagement do you take on?

Three flavours: full co-development (we embed alongside your team for the long haul), standalone art or design packages (a prop kit, a set of character concepts, a level), and short bursts of extra capacity around a crunch point or vertical slice. If you're not sure which you need, that's exactly what the first call is for.

How do you price work?

Per project, after scoping. Either fixed-price milestones or a day rate, whichever suits how your production runs. We'd rather quote accurately on a real brief than publish a rate card that fits nobody.

Which engines and tools do you work in?

Unity and Unreal Engine 5 on the engine side, with gameplay programmers covering both. Art runs through Blender, ZBrush, the Substance 3D suite and Photoshop. If your pipeline has house quirks, we adapt to yours, not the other way round.

Where are you based, and how do you communicate?

North East England, on UK time, working remote-first. You get one point of contact, regular check-ins in your tools (Slack, Discord, whatever you run), and honest progress updates, including the unglamorous ones.

Will you sign an NDA?

Happily, and before you share anything you'd rather keep quiet. Confidential projects stay confidential. You'll notice this page shows our own game, not client work.
